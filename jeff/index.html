ClientAPT

Your book. Their secrets.

A privacy-first appointment book for solo service providers, the tattoo artists, nail techs, stylists, and cleaners whose client list is itself confidential. It is the sole source of calendar truth: it never integrates with the system Calendar, never reaches the network, never enrolls in iCloud, never donates a Siri intent, never indexes to Spotlight. 03 · Engineering posture

How I choose to build.

Not dogma. A set of trade-offs I've made deliberately and can defend.

→

Native over cross-platform

C / Swift / Kotlin over Flutter, React Native, Electron: smaller binaries, lower battery and memory cost, direct platform APIs, no framework treadmill.

→

Tests are the contract

1:1 unit-test parity across platforms; drift is caught by audit prompts and logged, never papered over. 22 XCTest suites mirrored by 30 JUnit suites on my current work.

→

Zero third-party deps

Apple frameworks on iOS; AndroidX, Room, and Hilt on Android. No Firebase, Retrofit, Glide, or RxJava. Nothing to abandon me at the next OS release.

→

Data stays on device

Encrypted vaults, no silent network, tamper-evident SHA-256 audit chains. Privacy as an architecture decision, not a policy page.
